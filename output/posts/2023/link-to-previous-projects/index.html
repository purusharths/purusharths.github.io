Masterarbeit:

Abstract: In this work, we use the Finite Element model to simulate colorectal cancer progression using the Fischer KPP equation in the microenvironment of colonic crypts. Emphasizing the role of initial conditions and crypt geometry, the thesis shows their impact on in silico cancer development. We use a novel approach with Gaussian Random Fields to model heterogeneous cancer growth, showcasing varied influences under different covariances. Comparative analysis with histological images validate empirical estimations based on the rate of change of probabilities. Additionally, a stochastic approach identifies crypt areas vulnerable to cancer growth, offering a comprehensive view based on random fields and crypt geometry.

Full Text: https://heibox.uni-heidelberg.de/f/f6a8c4a3d57f44fab2ac/
